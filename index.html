Why Dragons

Dragons don’t need wings. The oldest dragons — the Vedic Ahi, the Chinese Long, the Aboriginal Rainbow Serpent — are serpentine. They swim through water, through earth, through consciousness itself. Wings were a European addition. The original dragon is a cosmic serpent.

Vedic

Rig Veda 1.32 calls Vritra “first-born of dragons” — a chaos serpent slain by Indra’s thunderbolt to free the world’s waters.

Chinese

The Long embodies yang energy — paired with phoenix (yin) to represent cosmic balance. Five claws for the emperor.

Aboriginal

The Rainbow Serpent created rivers and valleys as it moved across the land. Wingless. Older than any recorded mythology.

Cosmic

Jeremy Narby found that ayahuasca shamans worldwide see twin serpents resembling DNA’s double helix. The serpent is life itself.
